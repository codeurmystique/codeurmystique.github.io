Business Context

The bike-share company **Cyclistic** wanted to convert its casual riders into annual members. My task was to analyze data to understand the behavioral differences between the two groups and develop a strategy to increase memberships.

Data Analysis & Visualization

Using **R and RStudio**, I performed data cleaning and analysis on the bike-share dataset. I identified key usage patterns, such as ride duration and time of week, and then visualized these findings to highlight the distinctions between casual and member riders.

Key Insights

  • •

    Casual riders use bikes for much longer periods on average, suggesting they are on leisure rides rather than for commuting.

  • •

    Riding patterns for casual users are concentrated on weekends, while members ride consistently during weekdays, likely for commuting purposes.

  • •

    Docked bikes are favored by casual users, while members use a mix of classic and electric bikes, indicating different usage needs.

Recommendations

Based on these insights, I developed actionable recommendations to guide the marketing team's strategy:

  • •

    Launch a **"Weekend Explorer" marketing campaign** targeting casual riders, highlighting the cost savings of membership for longer, leisure trips.

  • •

    Introduce a **tiered membership option** that offers a discounted rate for weekend-only usage to attract this specific user base.

  • •

    Develop a feature in the mobile app that provides a **"cost saved" calculation** for casual riders, showing them how much they would have saved with a membership.
