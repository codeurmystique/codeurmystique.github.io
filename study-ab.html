Case Study 1: Employee Attrition Analysis

Business Problem: A human resources department was concerned about a high rate of employee attrition and wanted to identify key factors contributing to employees leaving the company.

Tools Used

  • •

    R (Data cleaning and predictive modeling)

  • •

    Tableau (Interactive dashboards)

  • •

    PowerPoint (Presenting key findings and recommendations)

Key Insights

  • •

    Employees with lower job satisfaction and higher workload are more likely to leave.

  • •

    Salary, while a factor, is not the primary driver of attrition for employees who have been with the company for more than 5 years.

  • •

    A lack of professional development opportunities is a significant predictor of attrition among junior-level staff.

Recommendations

  • •

    Implement a performance review system that includes regular check-ins on job satisfaction and workload management.

  • •

    Develop a mentorship program that pairs junior employees with senior staff to foster professional growth.

  • •

    Conduct exit interviews with departing employees to gather qualitative data on reasons for leaving and identify recurring patterns.
