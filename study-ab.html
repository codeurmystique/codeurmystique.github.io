Business Context

This study focused on employee turnover patterns at a company. Employee attrition is a significant issue that affects productivity, morale, and recruitment costs. The goal of this analysis was to identify the key factors contributing to employees leaving the company.

Data Analysis & Visualization

Using **Microsoft Excel**, I performed a deep dive into the company's HR dataset. I utilized pivot tables to summarize data by department, age, tenure, and salary. Pivot charts were created to visualize key trends and patterns, making the data easy to understand at a glance.

Key Insights

  • •

    High attrition was most prominent among younger employees and those in lower salary bands, suggesting a need for competitive compensation and growth opportunities.

  • •

    Work-life balance and job satisfaction were strongly correlated with retention, with employees who reported poor scores in these areas being more likely to leave.

  • •

    The Sales and HR departments had the highest turnover rates, indicating potential departmental-specific issues that needed to be addressed.

Recommendations

Based on the insights from the analysis, I provided actionable recommendations to help the company reduce attrition and improve retention:

  • •

    Implement **targeted retention programs** for early-career staff, including mentorship and career pathing.

  • •

    Review compensation in roles with high attrition to ensure salaries are competitive with the market.

  • •

    Promote **flexible working options** and work-from-home policies to improve work-life balance and overall job satisfaction.
